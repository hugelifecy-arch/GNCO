Methodology & Sources

This page explains how GNCO should be used and how coverage is maintained. GNCO remains informational prototype software.

Three-axis model (why “country = answer” is wrong)

  • Fund domicile: where the fund/vehicle is established.
  • Manager domicile: where the manager/adviser is regulated.
  • Investor locations: where marketing / distribution occurs.

GNCO should show outputs within this 3-axis context, not as universal “one-country advice”.

Evidence hierarchy

  • Primary: regulator websites, official law portals (e.g., EUR-Lex), official gazettes.
  • Secondary: regulator guidance/Q&A, official circulars, official handbooks.
  • Not acceptable alone: blogs, marketing pages, unverified summaries.

Coverage statuses

  • Supported — curated entry with sources + reviewed triggers.
  • Partial — informational but requires counsel for many scenarios.
  • Planned — placeholder; do not rely without verification.

“Requires counsel” triggers

  • Retail distribution / public offering
  • Cross-border marketing and any passporting/NPPR decisions
  • Complex derivatives/leverage, unusual liquidity features, side letters
  • Delegation / sub-advisory / outsourcing across borders

Update policy (recommended)

  • Cadence: re-verify each supported jurisdiction at least quarterly, and immediately after major regulatory updates.
  • Traceability: each entry has last_verified and links to primary sources.
  • Change log: record any content changes and why they were made.
  • Build metadata: inject build date + version hash into every public page.

See also: Coverage and Change log.

Important: GNCO is prototype software for informational purposes only. It is not an investment product and does not provide legal/tax/investment advice. Always verify outputs with qualified professionals. See Disclosures.
